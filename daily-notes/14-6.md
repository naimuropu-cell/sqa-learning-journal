# Severity vs Priority in Software Testing

## Introduction

Severity and Priority are two important concepts in Software Quality Assurance (SQA). They help teams decide how critical a defect is and how quickly it should be fixed.

## What is Severity?

Severity refers to the impact of a defect on the system or application.

### Examples:

* Application crash
* Payment failure
* Data loss

These issues have high severity because they affect core functionality.

## What is Priority?

Priority refers to how urgently a defect should be fixed.

### Examples:

* Typo on the homepage before a product launch
* Incorrect company logo on the landing page

These issues may have low severity but high priority because they affect business goals.

## Severity vs Priority

| Severity                        | Priority                                 |
| ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Measures impact of the defect   | Measures urgency of fixing the defect    |
| Decided mainly by QA            | Decided by QA, Product Owner, or Manager |
| Focuses on system functionality | Focuses on business needs                |

## Example Scenarios

### High Severity, High Priority

Payment gateway is not working.

### High Severity, Low Priority

A rarely used feature crashes in a low-traffic module.

### Low Severity, High Priority

A spelling mistake on the homepage during a marketing campaign.

### Low Severity, Low Priority

Minor UI alignment issue on an internal page.

## Conclusion

Severity indicates how serious a defect is, while Priority indicates how soon it should be fixed. Understanding both helps QA teams manage defects effectively and improve software quality.
