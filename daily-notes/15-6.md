# Bug Life Cycle in Software Testing

## Introduction

A Bug Life Cycle (Defect Life Cycle) describes the journey of a defect from the moment it is identified until it is closed. Understanding this process helps QA teams track, manage, and resolve issues efficiently.

## Stages of a Bug Life Cycle

### 1. New

A tester identifies a defect and reports it in the bug tracking system.

### 2. Assigned

The bug is assigned to a developer for investigation and fixing.

### 3. Open

The developer reviews the defect and starts working on it.

### 4. Fixed

The developer fixes the issue and marks the bug as fixed.

### 5. Retest

The QA engineer retests the functionality to verify the fix.

### 6. Verified

The defect is successfully fixed and works as expected.

### 7. Closed

The bug is officially closed after successful verification.

## Alternative States

### Reopened

If the issue still exists after fixing, the tester reopens the bug.

### Rejected

The developer determines that the reported issue is not a valid defect.

### Deferred

The defect is valid but its fix is postponed to a future release.

### Duplicate

The reported bug already exists in the tracking system.

## Simple Flow

New → Assigned → Open → Fixed → Retest → Verified → Closed

## Importance of Bug Life Cycle

* Improves communication between QA and developers
* Ensures defects are tracked properly
* Helps maintain software quality
* Prevents issues from being forgotten

## Conclusion

The Bug Life Cycle provides a structured process for managing defects. A good understanding of each stage helps QA engineers track issues effectively and contribute to delivering high-quality software.
